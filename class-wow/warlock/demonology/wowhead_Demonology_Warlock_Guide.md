# Demonology Warlock Major Cooldowns

## Demonic Tyrant
Summon Demonic Tyrant is a staple cooldown and drives lot of our damage sequences. It extends the time of just your Call Dreadstalkers when talented into Reign of Tyranny; however, Summon Demonic Tyrant gains an additional damage modifier for each demon summoned. Maximize this.

## Grimoires
Grimoire: Imp Lord provides us with an on-demand self-dispel when summoned and deals moderate damage for a two-minute cooldown. While it's on cooldown, you also gain access to Singe Magic, which can help dispel yourself and others in your party in a pinch. Grimoire: Fel Ravager is likely the more common option of the two simply because it will interrupt the target it's cast on, on demand, and when on cooldown, giving you access to another interrupt in the form of Spell Lock.

## Implosion
Our on-demand AoE is only available in the form of Implosion. It is a 15s cooldown and consumes a maximum of 6 Wild Imps to deal AoE damage. Note that this will consume any number of Wild Imps up to 6, so there is a world where you can implode the non-maximum number of Wild Imps. It is recommended to always implode with the 6 Wild Imp maximum whenever possible.

## Doomguard
A powerful AoE cooldown that can be used whenever there are multiple targets. Be aware that it does have a target cap of 5 enemies, so if you cast it with more than 5 enemies around, it will not deal damage to more than those five targets at any given time.

## Felstorm
For more advanced players, turning OFF auto-cast from Felstorm may be beneficial to your DPS in Single Target situations. This is primarily due to the energy it consumes that takes away from additional Legion Strike casts and a few melee attacks from your Felguard. This tip is EXTREMELY min-max and is not suitable for every player to use. If auto-cast is turned off and you are in a position to have it hit at least two targets but don't have your Felguard cast Felstorm, you will negate the other 'gains' you get by having it not auto-cast.

# Demonology Warlock Advanced Tips

## Demonic Cores
Since Demonic Core are our most common proc which enables us to use a more efficient way of building Soul Shards, with this in mind managing this resource is extremely important. Demonic Cores only stack to a maximum of 4 and there are periods where you will want to bank charges of this resource for cooldown windows. For example, you would want a minimum of 2 charges for each time you approach a Summon Demonic Tyrant cooldown as it will allow you to easily, and quickly, generate additional Soul Shards for more Wild Imps while your Demonic Tyrant is active, increasing its damage. 

Another example of effective use of Demonic Cores is during AoE where you can spread Doom to multiple targets for additional AoE since each application of Doom is its own damage source. 

Ideally, you will want to hold 2 charges of Demonic Core as often as possible. This is both a means of having quick resources available to you and allows you to not overcap your charges and waste procs.
