
# Destruction Warlock Overview
Destruction excels at dealing large hits through Chaos Bolt and strong cleave damage from Havoc. Our primary resource is Soul Shards, which we both generate and spend with several abilities in our kit. We can choose to either spend them on Chaos Bolt, a single-target ability, or on Rain of Fire, an AoE ability that deals damage over time. Destruction deals its damage in big hits and doesn't rely on pets doing the dirty work for us. That's still the case to this day, although we've become known for our signature cooldown, Summon Infernal, creating a powerful companion when summoned.

Destruction is by nature a relatively simple spec to play, but with a different Soul Shards system than Affliction and Demonology, the playstyle is quite different.

## Playing a Destruction Warlock
* **Soul Shard management:** Do not overcap Soul Shards. Destruction's Soul Shards resource is different than Affliction and Demonology, as all our shards are split into 10 soul shard fragments. We have great control over generating and spending this resource, so make sure you're aware of having Soul Shards when you need them, while not wasting any Soul Shards by overcapping.
* **Cooldown management:** Being able to properly manage your cooldowns, such as Havoc and Summon Infernal will make a big difference in how much damage you're doing. Making sure you have the correct amount of Soul Shards before using these, and making sure you don't lose out on any uses, is a big difference maker between high DPS and low DPS.
* **Movement:** When you're moving around, you only have very few ways to deal damage. You can play around with always casting abilities such as Conflagrate while running, but odds are you're going to lose out on DPS. That means, playing movement segments correctly will be a big difference maker. Utilizing abilities like Demonic Circle and Demonic Gateway properly will help you with that.

## Rotation Priority for Beginners
This is a very "down to the bone" rundown of the Destruction rotation.
* **Soul Shards:** We generate Soul Shard through our rotation with Immolate, Incinerate and Conflagrate.
* **Spenders:** Spend Soul Shard either with Chaos Bolt on single-target or Rain of Fire on AoE.
* **Cooldowns:** Summon Infernal is our big cooldown. During this cooldown, we deal extra damage and generate extra soul shards.

## Best Destruction Warlock Single Target Rotation
Destructions single-target follows a simple priority list. With Diabolist selected, the priority is as follows:
1. Maintain Immolate.
2. Cast Shadowburn when available, and if you are about to cap on soul shards.
3. Cast Chaos Bolt to avoid capping soul shards.
4. Cast Chaos Bolt if you have more than 4 soul shards and Infernal Bolt ready.
5. Soul Fire when available, and you have less than 4 soul shards.
6. Cast Conflagrate to keep this below 2 stacks or when moving.
7. Cast Conflagrate to generate soul shards and generate Backdraft stacks. Try to use these on Chaos Bolt as much as possible.
8. Cast Incinerate to generate soul shards.

## Best Destruction Warlock AoE Rotation
The Destruction AoE is very different depending on if you're playing Diabolist or Hellcaller. With Diabolist selected, the priority is as follows:
1. Maintain Immolate on your main target, as well as any target you expect lives longer than 10 seconds.
2. Spend your soul shards to avoid overcapping. 
3. Make sure you have no more than 2 soul shards before casting Infernal Bolt.
4. Cast Conflagrate to generate soul shards and Backdraft stacks.
5. Cast Incinerate to generate soul shards.

**Soul Shard Spending on AoE** depends on a few variables.
If you're playing Hellcaller, you want to cast Rain of Fire if there are 5+ targets up and you have Havoc active. If Havoc is not up, you spend soul shards with Rain of Fire on 3+ targets.
If Havoc is active, you spend soul shards with Chaos Bolt on up to 5 targets. Otherwise, you should only spend soul shards with Chaos Bolt on 1-2 targets.
Diabolist always spends soul shards on Chaos Bolt no matter what.

## Best Destruction Warlock Opener
1. Precast Soul Fire.
2. Pop Summon Infernal as well as any potions, trinkets and racials.
3. Cast Conflagrate to generate soul shards and Backdraft stacks.
4. Generate and spend as many soul shards as possible during your cooldowns. Try spend as many Backdraft stacks on casting Chaos Bolt as possible.
5. Proceed onto the normal priority list.

## Pre-Combat Check
Before any combat happens, it is important for Destruction Warlock to make sure the following are done. 
* **Get your food buff and oil applied!**
* **Summon your Pet!**
* **Drop healthstones!** - Cast Create Soulwell for every party/raid member to get a Healthstone.
* **Portals, portals everywhere!** - As needed, make sure your portals are down in an appropriate location. This includes both personal, Demonic Circle and group, Demonic Gateway portals.
* **Double Check your Talents!** - Have another quick look to make sure you are in the correct talents for the boss, and you can't see any changes that might help.

## Major Cooldown Usage
Destruction has a number of both big and small cooldowns that are important to know how to play in order to succeed.

### Summon Infernal
Summon Infernal is our 2-minute cooldown that helps us generate more Soul Shards during its duration.
* If you're playing Crashing Chaos, your Infernal cast also makes your damage more important. It's not only a point and click cooldown that does damage on its own; make sure you pair this up with any racial ability and trinkets if you can.
* Keep in mind you can delay this cooldown as long as you don't miss out on uses. During a Mythic+ dungeon you will likely want to pop this as often as possible because you will lose out on uses otherwise, but popping it at the correct time is important.

### Havoc
* Keep in mind you can generate 2x Soul Shards with Havoc while your spenders don't cost extra.
* Havoc only replicates 60% of the damage of your single-target abilities, meaning you should apply this to the less important target.
* Make sure your target is going to live for the full Havoc duration if possible.

### Diabolist Demons
When playing Diabolist Destruction, you can gain a good portion of damage by knowing what each of the demons summoned by the hero talent tree does. Because of the talent Touch of Rancora, and because of how Diabolist Destruction works, you want to trigger your demons by casting a Chaos Bolt. This is unless you're in a situation where you're damaging a lot of targets, and in that situation you should probably be playing Hellcaller.
* **Demonic Art: Overlord** - Charges your target and does a frontal cleave. Enemies hit by this cleave then takes increased damage from you and your demons because of the talent Cloven Souls. If the target you cast on to trigger the Demonic Art: Overlord dies before the overlord reaches the target, it instead swaps to a different target and hits that one. Keep this in mind, because if you're damaging a lot of targets, try not to trigger this onto a target that is standing alone. Try to aim this into a big pack for the most damage. Also, keep in mind your damage is increased to targets hit by this for 15 seconds, so try to hit those targets.
* **Demonic Art: Mother of Chaos** - Summons next to you and starts channelling damage into your target. Additionally, triggering this demonic art changes your next Incinerate into Infernal Bolt which deals increased damage and generates 2 full Soul Shards. Make sure you do not overcap on Soul Shards by casting this. Instead, cast Chaos Bolt to go down to at least 2,5 Soul Shards, but ideally below 2 Soul Shards before casting Infernal Bolt. Also, the ability Demonic Art: Mother of Chaos casts when summoned is a channelled AoE, which might not always start rechannelling onto a new target if the target dies during this channel. For that reason, try to cast this onto a target that's going to live for the full duration.
* **Demonic Art: Pit Lord** - Is also summoned right next to you, and starts dealing pulsating AoE damage onto and around your target. Additionally, triggering this Demonic Art turns your next Chaos Bolt into Ruination. A very powerful AoE around your target. Make sure you cast this on the mob where it's going to hit the most targets possible.

Some extra notes when palying Diabolist is, that you always cycle in the same order. It goes:
1. Overlord.
2. Mother of Chaos.
3. Pit Lord.

This resets on boss combat in raids, but not in dungeons. This means, you can play slower or faster from time to time, depending on what demon you want to summon. As an example, if you're in a dungeon and you're about to finish off a pack, just killing off the last few mobs, and you're about to gain Demonic Art: Pit Lord, do not trigger this on that pack. Instead, be a little patient and deal big damage on the next pack. Do not postpone this, because when playing, you do cycle through the rotation of demons fairly quickly. However, it adds a layer of complexity and depth to the playstyle and might ultimately make your damage output significantly higher.

### Malevolence
Before pressing Malevolence, always have all of your Wither applied. This can happen by you doing a big pull, casting Cataclysm -> Channel Demonfire and then following it up by Malevolence into either Chaos Bolt or Rain of Fire spam, collapsing your Wither stacks, dealing damage with Blackened Soul.

During Malevolence, all of the Soul Shards you cast grants double stacks of Wither to your targets, so try your very best to have some shards ready before, and make sure you dump as much as you can before Malevolence runs out. And be careful not to let your Wither drop on high stacks.
