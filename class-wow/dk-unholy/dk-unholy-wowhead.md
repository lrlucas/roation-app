
# Unholy Death Knight Rotation in Midnight Season 1

Unholy Death Knight primarily operates based on resources and current procs. This generally means that what we do at any given moment will be based on both of these factors, which makes a typical rotation out of the question, and instead operates on a priority list.

## Pre-Combat Check
Before entering combat, it is important for Unholy Death Knights to do the following. This applies to raids, dungeons, delves, or even open-world content:
* **Summon Timmy:** Make sure to summon your primary pet (Timmy) by casting Raise Dead.

## Core Principles
* **Keep Abilities on Cooldown:** Unholy is a very burst-focused specialization, which relies incredibly heavily on using its cooldowns as often as possible to maximize its damage. This makes cooldown setup and execution its highest priority.
* **Spend your Sudden Doom procs ASAP:** Sudden Doom has many interactions with Unholy talents, while also being the strongest non-cooldown button we can press in any scenario. This makes spending this proc with Death Coil or Epidemic as quickly as possible one of the best things you can do.
* **Keep an eye on your Ghouls:** Lesser Ghouls are the bread and butter of Unholy Death Knights in Midnight. So, making sure that you always have stacks of Lesser Ghoul from Festering Strike to allow Scourge Strike to summon them is vital.

## Best Unholy Death Knight Opener Priority
Unholy's Opener is fairly notorious for being a "nuclear launch sequence" to get going. This hasn't changed much in Midnight, though the sequences tend to be more simple than before.

2. Cast Festering Strike.

4. Cast Army of the Dead.

7. Use Combat Potion.

## Best Unholy Death Knight Single Target Rotation
Unholy in Single Target plays around a priority list, juggling both ability cooldowns and resources based on an order of power. This means with each GCD we go top to bottom on a list, and press the button with the current highest value.

### Priority Outside of Forbidden Knowledge
1. Cast Outbreak if Virulent Plague is not active.
2. Cast Army of the Dead on cooldown.
3. Cast Dark Transformation on cooldown.
4. Cast Soul Reaper on cooldown.
5. Cast Putrefy if Dark Transformation is active.
6. Cast Festering Scythe if its buff is about to expire.
7. Cast Death Coil if you have a Sudden Doom proc.
8. Cast Festering Strike if you have no Lesser Ghoul stacks.
9. Cast Scourge Strike if you have Lesser Ghoul stacks.
10. Cast Death Coil.

### Priority During Forbidden Knowledge
1. Cast Soul Reaper on cooldown.
2. Cast Putrefy on cooldown.
3. Cast Festering Scythe if its buff is about to expire.
4. Cast Necrotic Coil.
5. Cast Festering Strike if you have no Lesser Ghoul stacks.
6. Cast Scourge Strike if you have Lesser Ghoul stacks.

## Best Unholy Death Knight AoE Rotation
Unholy in AoE plays around a priority list, juggling both ability cooldowns and resources based on an order of power. 

### Priority Outside of Forbidden Knowledge
1. Cast Outbreak if Virulent Plague is not active.
2. Cast Army of the Dead on cooldown.
3. Cast Dark Transformation on cooldown.
4. Cast Festering Scythe if its buff is about to expire.
5. Cast Soul Reaper on cooldown.
6. Cast Putrefy if Dark Transformation is active.
7. Cast Death and Decay if you expect your targets to stay inside of it.
8. Cast Epidemic.
9. Cast Festering Strike if you have no Lesser Ghoul stacks.
10. Cast Scourge Strike if you have Lesser Ghoul stacks.

### Priority During Forbidden Knowledge
1. Cast Festering Scythe if its buff is about to expire.
2. Cast Putrefy on cooldown.
3. Cast Death and Decay if you expect your targets to stay inside of it.
4. Cast Soul Reaper on cooldown.
5. Cast Graveyard.
6. Cast Festering Strike if you have no Lesser Ghoul stacks.
7. Cast Scourge Strike if you have Lesser Ghoul stacks.

**AoE Further Details:** When in AoE scenarios, if Forbidden Knowledge is active, swap from using Necrotic Coil to using Graveyard at 6 or more targets. If Forbidden Knowledge is not active, instead swap to using Epidemic at 3 or more targets.

## Unholy Death Knight Major Cooldowns & Synergies

### Army of the Dead & Dark Transformation
* **Army of the Dead** is our biggest cooldown and serves as a trigger for many other talents (Raise Abomination, Summon Gargoyle, and Apocalypse Now).
* **Dark Transformation** also triggers several other talents, most importantly Commander of the Dead, Outnumber, and Ride or Die!.
* **Synchronization:** With the sheer quantity of pets being summoned, if you have talented Commander of the Dead, it's vital to ensure that Army of the Dead and Dark Transformation stay in sync, so you are using both at the same time. With Dark Transformation being an off-global cooldown ability, you can safely macro this into Army of the Dead and only have to worry about a single use between each Army window.
* **Add Spawns:** If you talent Outnumber, it becomes incredibly important to try to time your Dark Transformation with add spawns, as the Lesser Ghoul Sweeping Claws attacks are incredibly potent, and will constitute a major portion of our AoE damage potential.

### Putrefy & Soul Reaper Interactions
Putrefy is usually a short-duration cooldown, averaging about 15 seconds between uses, making it fairly rotational. However, due to Soul Reaper consuming 2 of your current Putrefy charges, it can be worth not pressing Putrefy if Soul Reaper will be available to use in the next 15 seconds or so. 

* **Execute Phase:** During Execute (Sub 35% enemy health), try not to press Putrefy. While your target is below 35% health, this will mean you never manually press Putrefy. Due to Soul Reaper casting Putrefy for you, this will save you both runes and globals.
* **Banking for Burst:** Outside of this, if you talent Reaping, you should try to bank all of your Putrefy charges for Dark Transformation windows. This will save you a global and a rune due to Soul Reaper casting Putrefy for you.
